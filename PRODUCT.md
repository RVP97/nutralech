# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary visitors read Spanish and are looking for trustworthy, personalized nutrition guidance: people considering an initial consultation, follow-ups, or remote plans; readers of the blog; and users of free calculators under `/herramientas` as an entry path. Locale and SEO center on Mexico (`es_MX`) while the site remains welcoming to Spanish speakers elsewhere. Context is often emotional (body image, health anxiety, chronic conditions) mixed with practical goals (fat loss, muscle, hormones, performance, relationship with food).

## Product Purpose

Nutralech is the public presence of Marialy Alonso: professional nutritional counseling, meal planning, education, and content (blog, Instagram) that supports sustainable habits and coordination with medical care when relevant. Success is measured by more qualified consult bookings, confident use of pricing and contact flows, and content that builds recognition and repeat visits without overpromising medical outcomes.

## Brand Personality

Warm, grounded, and scientifically literate: approachable copy, clear structure, and emphasis on personalization and balance rather than extremes. Three-word handle: **human**, **rigorous**, **sostenible**. Voice should feel like a careful professional in conversation, not a supplement ad or a cold clinic brochure.

## Anti-references

Avoid crash-diet urgency, before-and-after sensationalism, pseudo-science claims, and “miracle week” wellness tropes. Reject generic SaaS landing patterns that read as interchangeable AI output: twin stat cards with huge numbers and tiny labels as the main hero story, decorative glass and gradient chrome without intent, and stock “healthcare teal” clichés unless deliberately chosen with a named reference. Do not imply medical diagnosis or replace physicians; stay in nutrition counseling lane.

## Design Principles

Lead with trust and clarity before decoration: headline and primary action should answer who this is for and what to do next. Let photography and real credentials carry weight; motion and color earn their place. Keep calculator and checkout flows calm and legible; marketing warmth should not reduce form usability. Prefer specificity in copy (what happens in a session, what you coordinate with doctors) over repeated superlatives. Iterate against the “category reflex” test: if the palette and layout could be guessed from the word “nutrición” alone, push toward a more ownable scene and reference set.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 Level AA for text contrast, focus visibility, and form labels across marketing and tools. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for non-essential animation. Avoid relying on color alone for critical states; keep language inclusive around body size, food, and health conditions. Offer clear contact alternatives (email, WhatsApp) without trapping users in a single channel.
